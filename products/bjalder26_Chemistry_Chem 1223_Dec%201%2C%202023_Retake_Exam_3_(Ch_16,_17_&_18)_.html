12) You add 700 mL of a 0.160 M HI solution to 600 mL of a 0.120 M C6H5NH2 solution (Kb for C6H5NH2 is 3.8x10-10).  What will be the pH of that solution?
A) 1.40
B) 9.42
C) 9.23
D) 1.51*
Molarity strong acid
700 mL x (1L/1000mL) x (0.16 mol/L) = 0.112 mol HI
0.112 mol HI / 1.30 L = 0.0862 M

Molarity weak base
600 mL x (1L/1000mL) x (0.12 mol/L) = 0.0720 mol C6H5NH2
0.0720 mol C6H5NH2 / 1.30 L = 0.0554 M

Since there is more strong acid than weak base, the pH will be based on the remaining strong acid.
Subtract the molarity of weak base from the molarity of strong acid to get the remaining strong acid.
0.0862 M HI - 0.0554 M C6H5NH2 = 0.0308 M HI remaining
pH = -log(0.0308)
pH 1.51

14) If you have a 1.0 L buffer solution with 0.10 M acetic acid (Ka 1.75x10-5) and 0.10 M sodium acetate, then what will be the pH after adding 0.0600 moles of solid HCl (assume no change in volume)?
A) 5.36
B) 4.15*
C) 3.90
D) 4.76
Beginning moles:

1L x (0.10 mol sodium acetate/L) = 0.10 mol sodium acetate

Moles after adding 0.06 moles of HCl:
0.06 moles of HCl will react with 0.06 moles of acetate to produce 0.06 moles of acetic acid
0.10 mol acetic acid +0.06 mol = 0.160 mol acetic acid
0.10 mol sodium acetate -0.06 mol = 0.0400 mol acetic acid
pH = -log(1.75*10-5) + log(0.0400)/(0.160)
pH = 4.15

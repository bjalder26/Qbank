2) What is the oxidation state of S in SO42-?
A) +8
B) +2
C) +6*
D) +4

To determine the oxidation state of sulfur in SO42-, we consider the overall charge of the ion and the known oxidation states of other elements involved.

In the sulfate ion (SO42-), the oxygen atoms have a known oxidation state of -2. The overall charge of the ion is -2 as well. Since the sum of the oxidation states in an ion must equal the overall charge, we can calculate the oxidation state of sulfur.

Let's assume the oxidation state of sulfur as x. We have four oxygen atoms, each with an oxidation state of -2. Therefore:

(x) + 4(-2) = -2

Simplifying the equation:

x - 8 = -2

x = +6

6) What would be the molarity of a solution formed by dissolving 20.4 g of KBr in enough water to yield 400.0 mL of solution?
A) 0.000429 M
B) 23.3 M
C) 2.33x104 M
D) 0.429 M*

To calculate the molarity of a solution, we need to determine the number of moles of solute (KBr) and then divide it by the volume of the solution in liters.

First, let's calculate the number of moles of KBr:
Molar mass of KBr = 39.10 g/mol (atomic mass of K) + 79.90 g/mol (atomic mass of Br) = 119.00 g/mol
Moles of KBr = Mass of KBr / Molar mass of KBr = 20.4 g / 119.00 g/mol = 0.171 moles

Next, we convert the volume of the solution from milliliters to liters: Volume of solution = 400.0 mL x (1 L / 1000 mL) = 0.400 L

Finally, we calculate the molarity using the formula: Molarity (M) = Moles of solute / Volume of solution (in liters) Molarity = 0.171 moles / 0.400 L = 0.429 M

8) According to the following reaction, how many grams of KCl solution is required to react exactly with 41.5 mL of 0.172 M Zn(ClO3)2 solution?
2 KCl(aq) + Zn(ClO3)2(aq) → ZnCl2(s) + 2 KClO3(aq)
A) 0.532 g
B) 0.191 g
C) 1.06 g*
D) 0.0143 g

To determine the amount of KCl solution required to react with a given volume of Zn(ClO3)2 solution, we need to use the stoichiometry of the balanced chemical equation.
Given:
Volume of Zn(ClO3)2 solution = 41.5 mL
Molarity of Zn(ClO3)2 solution = 0.172 M
Molar mass of KCl = 74.55 g/mol

From the balanced chemical equation: 2 KCl(aq) + Zn(ClO3)2(aq) → ZnCl2(s) + 2 KClO3(aq)

First, calculate the number of moles of Zn(ClO3)2 in the given solution:
Moles of Zn(ClO3)2 = Volume (in liters) × Molarity
Volume of Zn(ClO3)2 solution = 41.5 mL × (1 L / 1000 mL) = 0.0415 L of Zn(ClO3)2
Moles of Zn(ClO3)2 = 0.0415 L × 0.172 M = 0.00714 moles

Since the stoichiometric ratio is 1:2 between Zn(ClO3)2 and KCl, we need twice the number of moles of KCl to react completely.
Moles of KCl = 0.00714 Moles Zn(ClO3)2 × (2 mol KCl / 1 mol Zn(ClO3)2) = 0.0143 moles

Finally, we can calculate the mass of KCl:
Mass of KCl = Moles of KCl × Molar mass of KCl
Mass of KCl = 0.0143 moles × 74.55 g/mol = 1.06 grams

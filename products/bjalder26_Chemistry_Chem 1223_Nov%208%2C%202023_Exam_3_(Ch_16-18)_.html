2) Which of the following chemicals are amphoteric?
A) SO2-
B) H2O*
C) NaOH
D) H3O+
Amphoteric (amph- like amphibian, means both) means that it can act as either an acid or a base.  What you should be looking for is a chemical with an acidic hydrogen (typically it will have the hydrogen listed first or a carboxylic acid group -COOH), that can also accept an additional hydrogen (e.g. water or negatively charged).  Thus H2O can be classified as amphoteric.

3) Which of the following is the strongest Lewis acid (best at giving up H+ or binding OH-)?
A) HF*
B) Na+
C) Li+
D) K+
The strongest acid, will have the weakest conjugate base.  The conjugate bases of Na+, Li+, and K+ are all strong bases (NaOH, LiOH, and KOH).  Thus the answer is HF.

14) What would be the pH of a solution formed by mixing 200 mL of 1.04 M NH4Cl with 200 mL of 0.194 M NH3?  The Kb for NH3 is 1.59x10-5.
A) 7.45
B) 8.47*
C) 9.93
D) 9.20
In this case, you're mixing a weak acid with it's weak conjugate base.
This will create a buffer solution, for which we will use the HendersonâHasselbalch equation to solve for pH.
First we need the moles of the acid and the base.
[NH4Cl] = 2 mL x (1 L/1000 mL) x (1.04 mol/1L) = 0.00208 mol
[NH3] = 2 mL x (1 L/1000 mL) x (0.194 mol/1L) = 0.000388 mol*
Ka = 10-14/Kb = 10^-14/(1.59x10-5)
pH = -log(Ka) + log(base/acid) = -log(6.29x10-10) + log(0.000388/0.00208)
pH = 8.47
*since we were using the same volume, we could have just plugged in molarity (before mixing) as a short-cut, or we could have calculated millimoles by not dividing by 1000, as a different shortcut.

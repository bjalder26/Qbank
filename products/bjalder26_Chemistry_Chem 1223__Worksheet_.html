1) (SLO 4.6) What is the [CH3CO2-]/[CH3CO2H] ratio necessary to make a buffer solution with a pH of 5.98? 
Ka = 1.8x10-5 for CH3CO2H.
A) 9.5 : 1
B) 17 : 1*
C) 14 : 1
D) 0.058 : 1
pH = -log(Ka) + log(base/acid)
5.98 = -log(1.8x10-5) + log([CH3CO2-]/[CH3CO2H])
5.98 = 4.74 + log([CH3CO2-]/[CH3CO2H])
5.98 - 4.74 = log([CH3CO2-]/[CH3CO2H])
1.24 = log([CH3CO2-]/[CH3CO2H])
101.24  = [CH3CO2-]/[CH3CO2H]
[CH3CO2-]/[CH3CO2H] = 17 : 1

2) (SLO 4.7) How many milliliters of 0.0732 M NaOH are required to titrate 35 mL of 0.158 M HBr to the equivalence point.
A) 75.5 mL*
B) 7.55 mL
C) 60.4 mL
D) 16.2 mL
35 mL x (1L/1000mL) x (0.158 mol/L) x (1 mol NaOH/1 mol HBr) x (1 L/0.0732 mol) x (1000 mL/1L) = 75.5 mL

2) According to the following reaction, how many grams of KCl solution is required to react exactly with 39.7 mL of 0.212 M Zn(ClO3)2 solution?
2 KCl(aq) + Zn(ClO3)2(aq) → ZnCl2(s) + 2 KClO3(aq)
A) 0.226 g
B) 0.627 g
C) 1.25 g*
D) 0.0168 g

To determine the amount of KCl solution required to react with a given volume of Zn(ClO3)2 solution, we need to use the stoichiometry of the balanced chemical equation.
Given:
Volume of Zn(ClO3)2 solution = 39.7 mL
Molarity of Zn(ClO3)2 solution = 0.212 M
Molar mass of KCl = 74.55 g/mol

From the balanced chemical equation: 2 KCl(aq) + Zn(ClO3)2(aq) → ZnCl2(s) + 2 KClO3(aq)

First, calculate the number of moles of Zn(ClO3)2 in the given solution:
Moles of Zn(ClO3)2 = Volume (in liters) × Molarity
Volume of Zn(ClO3)2 solution = 39.7 mL × (1 L / 1000 mL) = 0.0397 L of Zn(ClO3)2
Moles of Zn(ClO3)2 = 0.0397 L × 0.212 M = 0.00842 moles

Since the stoichiometric ratio is 1:2 between Zn(ClO3)2 and KCl, we need twice the number of moles of KCl to react completely.
Moles of KCl = 0.00842 Moles Zn(ClO3)2 × (2 mol KCl / 1 mol Zn(ClO3)2) = 0.0168 moles

Finally, we can calculate the mass of KCl:
Mass of KCl = Moles of KCl × Molar mass of KCl
Mass of KCl = 0.0168 moles × 74.55 g/mol = 1.25 grams

B) Cd(NO3)2
C) Cd
D) AgNO3

In the given reaction:

Cd(s) + 2 AgNO3(aq) → Cd(NO3)2(aq) + 2 Ag(s)

The element undergoing reduction is silver (Ag). Reduction is defined as the gain of electrons, and in this reaction, silver ions (Ag+) from silver nitrate (AgNO3) are being reduced to elemental silver (Ag), which is being formed as a solid.

  • Silver (Ag) is being reduced from Ag+ to Ag.
  • Cadmium (Cd) is being oxidized from Cd to Cd2+.

5) What is the oxidation state of S in SO42-?
A) +6*
B) +4
C) +8
D) +2

To determine the oxidation state of sulfur in SO42-, we consider the overall charge of the ion and the known oxidation states of other elements involved.

In the sulfate ion (SO42-), the oxygen atoms have a known oxidation state of -2. The overall charge of the ion is -2 as well. Since the sum of the oxidation states in an ion must equal the overall charge, we can calculate the oxidation state of sulfur.

Let's assume the oxidation state of sulfur as x. We have four oxygen atoms, each with an oxidation state of -2. Therefore:

(x) + 4(-2) = -2

Simplifying the equation:

x - 8 = -2

x = +6

Finally, we calculate the molarity using the formula: Molarity (M) = Moles of solute / Volume of solution (in liters) Molarity = =[22.6/119; 3] moles / =[20/100; 3] L = 0.950 M

1) Which of the following mixtures will NOT be homogeneous?
A) salt and water
B) vinegar and water
C) mud and water*
D) sugar and coffee

The correct answer is:

mud and water

Mud and water will not form a homogeneous mixture. When mud is added to water, it does not dissolve or mix uniformly. Instead, the mud particles will settle at the bottom, creating a heterogeneous mixture where the mud is not evenly distributed throughout the water.

2) Which of the following is a physical change?
A) Baking bread
B) Burning ethane
C) Dissolving magnesium chloride in water*
D) None of the above.
Dissolving magnesium chloride in water does not change the substance chemically, therefore it is a physical change.  State changes such as melting, freezing, boiling, condensing, and dissolving are physical changes.  Deforming or breaking an object is a physical change. 

Baking bread, and burning ethane involve creating new substances, so they are chemical changes.  "Burning", "lighting", "cooking", "baking", and "reacting" are all key words that indicate a chemical change.

4) Which of the following is an accurate description of systematic error?
A) Error that is random
B) Error that tends to be too high or tends to be too low*
C) Error that averages out over repeated trials
D) Error that has equal probability of being too high or too low

The accurate description of systematic error is:

Error that tends to be too high or tends to be too low

Systematic error refers to consistent and predictable deviations from the true value in the same direction. It occurs due to flaws or biases in the measurement process and leads to measurements that are consistently either higher or lower than the true value. This type of error does not average out over repeated trials and can significantly affect the accuracy of the measurements.

10) Which of the following describes a homogeneous mixture?
A) two or more elements that are not covalently or ionically bonded and have a uniform composition*
B) two or more elements or compounds that are not covalently or ionically bonded and do not have a uniform composition
C) two or more atoms covalently bonded together
D) two or more elements bonded to each other

An atom: A fundamental unit of matter that consists of a nucleus (containing protons and neutrons) and electrons orbiting the nucleus.

A molecule: Two or more atoms covalently bonded together. It can be a compound or a diatomic molecule composed of the same type of atoms.

A compound: Two or more different elements chemically bonded together. Compounds have a fixed ratio of elements and can be represented by a chemical formula.

A heterogeneous mixture: Two or more elements or compounds that are not covalently or ionically bonded and do not have a uniform composition. In a heterogeneous mixture, the components are not evenly distributed, and their properties may vary within the mixture.

A homogeneous mixture: Two or more elements or compounds that are not covalently or ionically bonded and have a uniform composition. In a homogeneous mixture, the components are evenly distributed throughout, and the mixture appears uniform at the macroscopic level.

To summarize:

  • Atom: Fundamental unit of matter.
  • Molecule: Two or more atoms covalently bonded together.
  • Compound: Two or more different elements chemically bonded together.
  • Heterogeneous mixture: Components are not evenly distributed and do not have a uniform composition.
  • Homogeneous mixture: Components are evenly distributed and have a uniform composition.

11) An experiment to determine the density of a sugar solution garners the following results: 1.06 g/mL, 1.82 g/mL, 2.05 g/mL, 1.76 g/mL. If the actual value for the density of the sugar solution is 1.68 g/mL, which statement below best describes her results?
A) The results are accurate but not precise.*
B) The results are accurate and precise.
C) The results are precise but not accurate..
D) The results are neither accurate nor precise.

To analyze the results, we need to understand the concepts of accuracy and precision. Accuracy refers to how close the measured values are to the true or known value, while precision refers to how close the measured values are to each other.

Given the known value for the density of the  sugar solution is  1.68 g/mL, let's assess the results:

1.06 g/mL, 1.82 g/mL, 2.05 g/mL, 1.76 g/mL

Looking at the values, we can see that the average is very close to the actual value. This indicates a high level of accuracy, as the average of the measured values closely aligns with the known value.

However, the measured values are not very close to each other, indicating a low level of precision.

Based on this analysis, the best statement that describes the results is:

The results are accurate but not precise.

The measured values are not precise because they are not very close to each other, but they are accurate because their average is close to the the known value.

12) Read the water level (shown in mL) with the correct number of significant figures. 

A) 2.75 mL

  1. Start by identifying the main intervals or markings on the graduated cylinder. For example, if the cylinder has markings every 1 mL, those are the main intervals.

  2. Observe the position of the meniscus (the curved surface of the liquid) within the main interval. Determine if the meniscus is closer to the lower or upper marking of the interval.  In this case the meniscus appears to be a bit above the 2.6 mL mark.

  3. Next, mentally divide the main interval into smaller divisions. Estimate the position of the meniscus within these smaller divisions. For example, if you are estimating to one decimal place, divide the main interval into 10 equal parts.

  4. Based on your judgment, determine the fraction or proportion of the interval that the meniscus appears to be closer to. Estimate this fraction as accurately as possible.  In this case, it might be reasonable to estimate another 0.05 (exactly in the middle of the 2.6 and 2.7 mL marks) or 0.06 mL (just above  the middle of the 2.6 and 2.7 mL marks).

  5. Add the estimated fraction to the main interval reading. This will give you an estimate of the measurement with an extra significant figure.  Reasonable estimates could include 2.65 mL or 2.66 mL, however only 2.65 is an option choice.
The main concept being tested on is that you need to estimate one additional place value past what the main intervals show.

Protons: Protons carry a positive charge of +1 and are located in the nucleus of an atom. The approximate mass of a proton is 1.67 x 10^-27 kilograms or 1 atomic mass unit (amu).

Neutrons: Neutrons are electrically neutral, meaning they have no charge. Like protons, they are located in the nucleus of an atom. The approximate mass of a neutron is also 1.67 x 10^-27 kilograms or 1 atomic mass unit (amu), which is very similar to the mass of a proton.

Electrons: Electrons carry a negative charge of -1 and orbit around the nucleus of an atom in electron shells. The mass of an electron is much smaller compared to protons and neutrons. It is approximately 9.11 x 10^-31 kilograms or about 1/1836 amu.

A) 5.61 g
B) 1.33x104 g
C) 0.00272 g
D) 367 g*

To calculate the mass of a given number of atoms, we need to multiply the moles of the element by the molar mass of the element.

The number of atoms in one mole is 6.022 x 1023.

The molar mass of Zn is approximately 65.41 g/mol.

First, let's calculate the moles of Zn using Avogadro's number: moles = number of atoms / Avogadro's number moles = 3.38 × 1024 atoms × \( \frac{1 mol}{6.022 x 10^{23} atoms} \) ≈ 5.61 mol

Next, we can calculate the mass: mass = moles × molar mass mass = 5.61 mol × 65.41 g/mol mass ≈ 367 g

16) What is the name of the Fe isotope that has 34 neutrons?
A) Iron-26
B) Iron-59
C) Iron-60.*
D) Iron-34

To determine an isotope given its chemical symbol and the number of neutrons, follow these steps:

  1. Identify the chemical symbol: The chemical symbol represents the element. It is typically a one- or two-letter abbreviation, such as H for hydrogen, C for carbon, or Au for gold.

  2. Determine the atomic number: The atomic number of an element corresponds to the number of protons in the nucleus of its atoms. It is usually listed as a subscript to the left of the chemical symbol. You can find the atomic number on the periodic table.

  3. Calculate the mass number: The number of neutrons is given. Add this number to the atomic number to determine the mass number. The mass number represents the total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus.

  4. Write the isotope: Once you know the atomic number and the number of neutrons, you can write the isotope by combining the chemical symbol, atomic number, and mass number. The atomic number is written as a subscript, and the mass number is written as a superscript.

In this problem:

  1. The chemical symbol is Fe.
  2. The atomic number of iron is 26.
  3. Add the number of neutrons (34 neutrons) to the atomic number (26) to determine the mass number: 34 + 26 = 60..
  4. Write the isotope: Iron-60.

Abundance of second isotope = 15.6%
Abundance of third isotope = 2.22%

To find the abundance of the lightest isotope, we subtract the abundances of the other two isotopes from 1: Abundance of lightest isotope = 1 - (abundance of second isotope + abundance of third isotope) = 1 - (0.156+ 0.0222) = 0.822

Now, let's calculate the average atomic mass.
Average atomic mass = (mass of first isotope * abundance of first isotope) + (mass of second isotope * abundance of second isotope) + (mass of third isotope * abundance of third isotope)
                 = (25.511 amu x 0.822) + (27.652 amu x 0.156) + (29.683 amu x 0.0222)
                 = 20.965) amu + 4.3137 amu + 0.65896 amu
                 = 25.938 amu

Rounded to the appropriate number of significant figures, the average atomic mass of the element is approximately 25.9 amu.

1) A sealed container contains 0.541 L of water with an atmosphere of oxygen gas. What is the concentration of O2 in the water if the external pressure is 5.43 atm given that kH for O2 is 1.66 × 10-6 M/mm Hg at this temperature?
A) 4300 (3 sf) M
B) 9.01x10-6 M
C) 1.19x10-8 M
D) 0.00685 M*

Step 1: Convert the external pressure from atm to mm Hg: 5.43 atm x 760 mmHg / 1atm ≈ 4130 mm Hg

Step 2: Calculate the concentration of O2 in water using Henry's law:
Concentration of O2 = kH * Partial pressure of O2
Partial pressure of O2 = External pressure

Concentration of O2 = 1.66 × 10-6 M/mm Hg x  4130 mm Hg mm Hg
Concentration of O2 ≈ 0.00685 M

6) The enthalpy of solution for a compound is -49 kJ/mol, and the enthalpy of hydration for the compound's ion X- is -250 kJ/mol. Calculate the lattice energy (in kJ/mol) of the compound.
A) -201 kJ/mol
B) -299 kJ/mol
C) 201 kJ/mol*
D) 299 kJ/mol

To solve this problem, we can use the following equation:

ΔHsolution = ΔHlattice + ΔHhydration

Where: ΔHsolution = Enthalpy of solution
ΔHlattice = Lattice energy
ΔHhydration = Enthalpy of hydration

We are given: ΔHsolution = -49 kJ/mol
ΔHhydration = -250 kJ/mol

Rearranging the equation, we can solve for ΔHlattice:

ΔHlattice = ΔHsolution - ΔHhydration

Plugging in the given values:

ΔHlattice = -49 kJ/mol - (-250 kJ/mol) = -49 kJ/mol + 49.0 kJ/mol = 201 kJ/mol

16) (SLO 1.6) What is the change in boiling point of a solution of 34.2 g Na3PO4 (163.9 g/mol) in 765 g of water? Kb for water is 0.512°C/m.  Assume ideal behavior.
A) 0.559°C*
B) 22.9°C
C) 0.000140°C
D) 0.140°C

To calculate the change in boiling point of a solution, we can use the equation:

ΔTb = i x Kb x m

where: i = van't Hoff factor
ΔTb = change in boiling point
Kb = boiling point elevation constant (0.512 °C/m for water)
m = molality of the solution (moles of solute per kilogram of solvent)

First, we need to calculate the molality of the solution, which is the number of moles of solute per kilogram of solvent.

 34.2 g Na3PO4 x (1 mol Na3PO4)/(163.9 g Na3PO4) = 0.209 mol Na3PO4

Step 2: Calculate the molality of the solution: Molality = moles of solute / mass of solvent (in kg) Mass of solvent (water) = 765 g x 1 kg/1000 g = 0.765 kg 

Molality =0.209 mol Na3PO4 mol / 0.765 kg ≈ 0.273  M

Step 3: van't Hoff factor
The van't Hoff factor for Na3PO4 will be estimated at 4 because  Na3PO4 contains 4 ions.

Step 3: Calculate the change in boiling point: ΔTb = i x Kb x m ΔTb = 4 x 0.512 °C/m * 0.273  M ≈ 0.559  M °C

17) (SLO 1.1) The osmotic pressure of a solution formed by dissolving 39.7 mg of Aspirin (C9H8O4) (180.16 g/mol) in enough water to make 0.227 L of solution at 40°C is _____ atm.
A) 0.00319
B) 24.9
C) 0.0249*
D) 0.00566

Given: Mass of Aspirin = 39.7 mg x 1 g/1000 mg= 0.0397 g
Molar mass of Aspirin (C9H8O4) = 180.16 g/mol
Volume of solution (V) = 0.227 L

First, calculate the number of moles of Aspirin:

moles of Aspirin = 0.0236 g x (1mol Aspirin)/(180.16 g Aspirin) ≈ 0.000220 mol

Next, calculate the molarity (M) of the solution:

Molarity (M) = moles of solute / volume of solution Molarity (M) = 0.0000220 mol / 0.227 L
Molarity (M) ≈  0.000971 M

Now we can calculate the osmotic pressure:

π = MRT

where: M is the molarity of the solution R is the ideal gas constant (0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K)) T is the temperature in Kelvin (K)

Given: Temperature (T) = 40°C +273.15 = 313.15 K

π = (0.000971) * (0.08206 L·atm/(mol·K)) * (313.15 K) π ≈ 0.0249 atm

20) (SLO 1.3) The Henry’s law constant for hydrogen gas in water at 20ºC is 8.1 x 10-4 M/atm. When the partial pressure of hydrogen above a sample of water is 0.743 atm, the concentration of hydrogen in the water is ______ M.
A) 6.02
B) 917
C) 0.00109
D) 0.000602*

Henry's law relates the concentration of a gas in a liquid to its partial pressure above the liquid. The equation for Henry's law is:

C = k * P

where: C is the concentration of the gas in the liquid (in Molarity, M) k is the Henry's law constant for the specific gas-solvent system (in M/atm) P is the partial pressure of the gas above the liquid (in atm)

Given: Henry's law constant (k) for hydrogen gas in water = 8.1 x 10-4 M/atm
Partial pressure of hydrogen (P) = 0.743 atm

Using Henry's law, we can calculate the concentration of nitrogen gas in the water (C):

C = (8.1 x 10-4 M/atm) * (0.743 atm) C ≈ 0.000602 M

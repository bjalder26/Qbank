1) (SLO 4.3) Identify the strongest acid.
A) HClO4*
B) HClO
C) HClO2
D) HClO3
The oxyacid with the most oxygens will be the strongest acid, because it's conjugate base will be the most stable due to resonance.

2) (SLO 4.4) Calculate the pOH of a solution that contains 8.75x10-4 M H3O+ at 25°C
A) 3.06
B) 10.9*
C) 1.14x10-11
D) 4.00
pH = -log[H+]
pH = -log(8.75x10-4
pH = 3.06
pOH = 14 - 3.06
pOH = 10.9

3) (SLO 4.2) Which of the following is a Arrhenius base?
A) H2
B) CH2CH2
C) Be(OH)2*
D) HCl
E) None of the above.
HCl is an acid.
H2 will not dissociate in water, and is not an acid or a base.
CH2CH2 does not have an acidic hydrogen (the C-H bonds won't break), and is not an acid or a base.
Be(OH)2 is the only base.

5) (SLO 4.1) Which of the following is a Brønsted–Lowry acid?
A) CHCH
B) LiOH
C) NH3
D) HClO2*
E) All of the above.
You can tell HClO2 is a Brønsted–Lowry acid, by the H in the front of the formula.
NH3 is a base.
CHCH does not have an acidic hydrogen (the C-H bonds won't break).
LiOH is a base.

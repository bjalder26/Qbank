2) (SLO 4.3) Identify the weakest acid.
A) They are all equal strength
B) H2SO4
C) H2SO3
D) H2SO2*
The oxyacid with the fewest oxygens will be the weakest acid, because it's conjugate base will be the least stable due to resonance.

4) (SLO 4.1) Which of the following is a Brønsted–Lowry acid?
A) CH3NH2
B) H3PO4*
C) CH4
D) Mg(OH)2
E) All of the above.
You can tell H3PO4 is a Brønsted–Lowry acid, by the H in the front of the formula.
CH3NH2 is a base.
CH4 does not have an acidic hydrogen (the C-H bonds won't break).
Mg(OH)2 is a base.

5) (SLO 4.2) Which of the following is a Brønsted–Lowry base?
A) CHCH
B) LiOH*
C) HClO2
D) Cl2
E) None of the above.
HClO2 is an acid.
Cl2 will not dissociate in water, and is not an acid or a base.
CHCH does not have an acidic hydrogen (the C-H bonds won't break), and is not an acid or a base.
LiOH is the only base.
